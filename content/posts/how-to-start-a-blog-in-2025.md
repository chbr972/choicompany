Blogging is not dead. The blogs that died were the ones chasing algorithms instead of readers. The ones that survived — and thrive — built genuine value around real expertise.

Here's how to start one in 2025.

## Step 1: Pick a Niche You Can Sustain

The most common blogging mistake is choosing a niche based purely on search volume or advertiser rates, with no real connection to the topic.

You'll be writing about this for months or years. You need genuine interest or expertise — ideally both.

**Good niche criteria:**
- You have real knowledge or strong curiosity about the topic
- There's an audience actively searching for this information
- You can write at least 50 posts on it without running dry

**Finding your niche:**
- List everything you know better than most people
- List everything you spend hours reading or watching voluntarily
- Cross-reference with keyword research tools (Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs free tools)

## Step 2: Choose Your Platform

For a monetizable, SEO-serious blog, self-hosted WordPress or a Next.js static site are the top choices.

**WordPress** is the safe choice: huge ecosystem, extensive plugins, easy for non-developers.

**Next.js/static sites** offer better performance, simpler hosting (Vercel's free tier), and full control — but require some technical comfort.

Avoid hosted platforms (Substack, Medium) as your primary platform if ad revenue is the goal. You don't own the real estate.

## Step 3: Set Up the Technical Basics Right

Before publishing a word:

- **Domain**: Pick something memorable, brandable, and .com if possible
- **Hosting**: Vercel, Netlify, or WP Engine for speed
- **SSL**: Non-negotiable in 2025
- **Analytics**: Google Analytics 4 from day one — you want historical data
- **Search Console**: Connect Google Search Console immediately

## Step 4: Write for Humans First, Algorithms Second

SEO is important. It's not more important than writing clearly and helpfully.

**Structure every post with:**
- A strong hook that earns the next paragraph
- Subheadings that let scanners find what they need
- Concrete examples, not just abstractions
- A clear takeaway or call to action at the end

**Keyword research simplified:**
1. Find the question your target reader is actually asking
2. Search that question in Google
3. Note what format ranks (listicles? Deep guides? Videos?)
4. Write something better

## Step 5: Commit to a Publishing Cadence

Consistency beats volume. One high-quality post per week is better than five mediocre ones.

The algorithm rewards freshness and consistency. So does your own skill development — writing is a craft that improves with repetition.

Build a content calendar three months ahead. When you're struggling for ideas, past planning saves you.

## Step 6: Monetize in Layers

Ad revenue requires traffic. Build the audience first, then layer monetization.

**Traffic milestones and corresponding monetization:**
- 1,000 monthly sessions: Apply for Google AdSense (minimum viable)
- 10,000 monthly sessions: Consider Ezoic or Mediavine entry
- 50,000+ monthly sessions: Mediavine full access, direct sponsorships
- 100,000+ monthly sessions: Premium ad networks, serious sponsorship revenue

**Beyond ads:**
- Affiliate marketing (relevant product recommendations)
- Digital products (templates, ebooks, courses)
- Email list → newsletter sponsorships

## Step 7: Build an Email List from Day One

Social platforms come and go. Your email list is an asset you own.

Put a simple signup form on every page. Offer something genuinely useful in exchange — a useful checklist, template, or mini-guide.

Even 500 engaged subscribers is a meaningful asset.

---

Blogging is a long game. Most blogs that succeed took 12–18 months to gain real traction. The ones that fail give up at month three.

Write well. Write consistently. Serve readers first. The traffic and revenue follow.
