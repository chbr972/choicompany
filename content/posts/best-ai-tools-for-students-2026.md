## 1. Claude — Best for Writing and Understanding Concepts

For essay writing, literature analysis, history papers, and any subject requiring clear writing, Claude is the best AI tool available.

**What students use Claude for:**
- Explaining difficult concepts in plain language
- Getting feedback on essay drafts (not writing them for you)
- Breaking down complex academic texts
- Creating study outlines from course materials
- Practicing debate arguments from multiple angles

**A better approach to using Claude for essays**: Don't ask Claude to write your essay. Ask it to explain the concepts, critique your arguments, suggest counterarguments, or review your structure. **Price**: Free tier is sufficient for most student use.

## 2. NotebookLM — Best for Studying from Your Own Materials

NotebookLM is Google's document-grounded AI — you upload your notes, textbooks, and lecture slides, and it answers questions based only on those materials.

**Why students love NotebookLM:**
- Audio Overview: Turn your notes into a podcast-style discussion — great for auditory learners
- Ask questions about uploaded course materials
- Generate study guides and practice questions from your notes
- Summarize long readings in minutes — or use our [free AI text summarizer](/tools/ai-text-summarizer) for quick summaries of any article or document
- Completely free

**Perfect use case**: Upload all your readings for a unit, then ask NotebookLM to create a comprehensive study guide. Ask it to quiz you on key concepts. Use the Audio Overview to review while commuting.

**Price**: Free. No limits for students.

## 3. Perplexity AI — Best for Research with Sources

Unlike general AI chatbots, Perplexity retrieves information from the web and cites every claim. For research assignments where you need real sources, it's invaluable.

**Why Perplexity is better for research:**
- Cites sources you can actually use in bibliography
- Real-time information (not limited to training data)
- Follow-up questions build deeper understanding
- Faster than Googling for complex questions

**Important caveat**: Always click through to verify citations. AI can occasionally misattribute or paraphrase inaccurately. Treat Perplexity as a research starting point, not a final source.

**Price**: Free for most student use. Pro at $20/month for heavy research users.

## 4. ChatGPT — Best for General Problem-Solving

ChatGPT's versatility makes it one of the most useful tools for students across all subjects.

**What students use ChatGPT for:**
- Getting step-by-step explanations of math and science problems
- Debugging code for CS courses
- Translating and understanding foreign language texts
- Generating practice problems
- Practicing for oral exams (it'll act as an interviewer)

**For CS students**: ChatGPT explains code and debugging errors clearly and is excellent for learning new programming languages.

**Price**: Free tier with GPT-4o (limited); Plus at $20/month for heavier use.

## 6. Photomath — Best for Math

Photomath lets you point your phone camera at a math problem and get a step-by-step solution. It works across arithmetic, algebra, geometry, trigonometry, and calculus.

**Why students use Photomath:**
- Instant answers from your camera
- Step-by-step explanations (not just answers)
- Works offline
- Handwritten equation support

**Price**: Free (basic); Plus at $9.99/month for detailed explanations.

## 7. Wolfram Alpha — Best for Science and Math Depth

Wolfram Alpha is the most powerful computational knowledge engine available. It's not an AI chatbot — it's a specialized engine for mathematics, science, statistics, and factual queries.

**Best use cases:**
- Complex calculus and differential equations
- Chemistry equations and molecular data
- Physics formulas and unit conversions
- Statistics and data analysis
- Factual lookups (demographics, historical data)

ChatGPT and Claude can help with math, but Wolfram Alpha gives you verified, computational answers with visual plots.

**Price**: Free basic access; Pro at $7.99/month.

## Tips for Using AI Tools in School

**Use AI to understand, not just to answer**: Ask for explanations of concepts, not just answers. You learn the material; AI doesn't take the exam for you.

**Verify everything**: AI tools can produce confident-sounding errors. Always check important facts, especially for academic submissions.

**Check your school's AI policy**: Academic integrity policies on AI use vary widely. Know what's allowed before using AI for assignments.

**Use AI for the hard parts**: Editing, research, concept explanations — these are where AI adds the most value for students without crossing academic integrity lines.
